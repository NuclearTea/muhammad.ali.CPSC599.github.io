Secret Morse Encoder

Using some basic electronic hardware, and a computer, you too can build a simple Morse Code Encoder which can convert your plain text into flashing lights in Morse.

What you will need:

  • A computer
  • Arduino or other microcontroller, I use an Arduino Leonardo
  • Serial Terminal, I am using the built in Arduino Serial Terminal
  • Appropriate USB Cable for your microcontroller
  • Rotary Encoder, I am using the KY-040 Rotary Encoder
  • Dual Axis Joystick Module, I am using the KY-023 Joystick Module
  • Breadboard
  • Any Colour LED
  • Resistor
  • 9 F-M Cables
  • 4 M-M Cables

Steps to Powering Components

  1. First we will give power to the Breadboard and ground it. To achieve this, we will use 1 M-M Cable to power the positive length and 1 M-M Cable to ground the negative length of the board.

    • Using 1 M-M Cable, connect one end into the microcontrollers ground pin and the other into the Breadboards negative length section.
    • Using another 1 M-M Cable, connect 1 end to the 5V5 Pin into the microcontroller and the other into the positive length of the Breadboard.

  2. Next, we will connect the Joystick to the Breadboard and Arduino. This will require minimum 3 F-M Cables (if you would like to use both directions and the press in button, they will require 1 more F-M Cable each).

    • Using 1 F-M Cable, connect the female end into the GND Pin of the Joystick and the Male end into the the same negative line on the Breadboard.
    • Using 1 F-M Cable, connect the female end into the +5V Pin of the Joystick and the Male end into the same positive line on the Breadboard.
    • Using 1 last F-M Cable, connect the female end into the VRy Pin of the Joystick and the Male end into the A0 (Analog Pin 0 on the Arduino) Pin.

  3. Next, we will connect the Rotary Encoder to the Arduino. This will require 5 F-M Cables.

    • First, we will connect the female end into thte GND Pin of the Rotary Encoder and the Male end into the same negative line on the Breadboard.
    • Second, we will connect the female end into the + Pin of the Rotary Encoder and the Male end into the same positive line on the Breadboard.
    • Third, we will connect the female end into the SW Pin of the Rotary Encoder and the Male end into the Digital Pin 8 on the Arduino.
    • Fourth, we will connect the female end of the DT Pin of the Rotary Encoder and the Male end into Digital Pin 7 on the Arduino.
    • Fifth, we will connect the female end into the CLK Pin of the Rotary Encoder and the Male end into Digital Pin 6 on the Arduino.

  4. Finally, easiest for last, we will connect an LED to the Arduino.

    • First, attch the LED to the Breadboard, make sure to note the pin which is longer, this is **Important**. Ensure that the legs are on differnt rows.
    • On the same row as the **Long Leg** of the LED, place a resister of 220 ohms in between the LED and the Positive and Negative lengths of the Breadboard. Only one of its legs should be on the same row of the led, the other leg should be on a row with nothing.
    • Using 1 M-M Cable, connect 1 end to Digital Port 3 on the Arduino, and the other end to a pin on the same row as the **Long Leg** of the LED. This Row should now have a leg of the resister, leg of an LED, and 1 pin.
    • Using 1 M-M Cable, connect 1 end to a pin on the same row as the **Short Leg** of the LED and the other end to the negative length of the breadboard.
